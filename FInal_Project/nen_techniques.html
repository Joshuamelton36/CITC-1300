What to expect?

This page is dedicated to expanding upon the basics of nen, explaining the more complicated techniques employed in Hunter Hunter, all of these techniques are founded on the three fundamental principles, Ten, zetsu, and Ren. Using all three in combinaation for devestating results.

Combination Principles

As explained above, these principles host a variety of the fundamentals, fusing them into an effort to create a more powerful applications of nen.

Gyo

Gyo is formed by using Zetsu and Ten to concentrate aura in the eyes to vastly improve vision and protect against In, without Gyo one cannot see In.

Ko

Ko is concentrating ones aura into their limbs and using ren to enheance it, vastly increasing their offensive power but dropping their defensive strength all together.

Ken

Ken: Ken is an advanced defensive technique that encompasses Ten, Ren, and Zetsu. It involves maintaining a constant flow of aura around the entire body while simultaneously enhancing its intensity. This creates a powerful defensive barrier that offers comprehensive protection against both physical and Nen-based attacks.

In

Basically allows the user to make their aura invisible, primarily used to trick opponenets while using Hatsu.

En

En: En is a technique that expands the user's aura around them in a large sphere, effectively extending their sensory range. By combining Ren and Ten, users can create a more potent and expansive En, allowing them to detect subtle changes in their surroundings and perceive the presence of others from a greater distance.

Shu

Shu is a technique that involves enveloping an object with one's aura to enhance its properties or abilities. By combining Ren and Ten, users can strengthen their Shu, making objects more durable, sharper, or imbued with Nen-based effects. Shu is commonly used to empower weapons or tools for combat or utility purposes.
